**Advantages of SOAP:**

- **Formal Contracts:** SOAP uses the Web Services Description Language (WSDL) to define
  the service's interface, ensuring that all parties adhere to a specific contract, which
  reduces
  misinterpretation. ([Nordic APIs](https://nordicapis.com/common-cases-when-using-soap-makes-sense/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

- **Protocol Independence:** SOAP is not restricted to transmitting over HTTP; it can
  operate over various protocols such as SMTP, JMS, or FTP, providing flexibility in
  communication
  methods. ([blog.hubspot.com](https://blog.hubspot.com/website/rest-vs-soap?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

- **Built-in Error Handling:** SOAP has a built-in error handling mechanism that allows
  for detailed error reporting, helping developers diagnose and resolve issues more
  efficiently and improving the overall reliability of the communication
  process. ([phoenixNAP | Global IT Services](https://phoenixnap.com/glossary/what-is-soap?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Considerations:**

While SOAP offers these benefits, it is also known for being more complex and having
higher overhead compared to RESTful APIs. As a result, many modern web services prefer
REST for its simplicity and efficiency. However, in scenarios where security, formal
contracts, and transaction reliability are paramount, SOAP remains a valuable choice.

In summary, despite the rise of RESTful APIs, SOAP continues to be utilized by companies,
particularly in sectors where its robust features align with specific operational
requirements.
